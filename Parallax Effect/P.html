SURFING

India’s 7500 kilometre coastline provides several spots for surfboarding. The largest waves are typically seen between May and September, the pre-monsoon and monsoon season. Waters of India are already well-liked for hosting a range of adventure sports like rafting, kayaking, sailing, stream crossing, and plenty of others. One such upcoming sport is Surfing in India. The western coast is great for beginners to get acquainted with a surfboard, whereas eastern shores have stronger currents and harder wave breakpoints. Surfing in India is growing in quality, and there are some great spots along the country’s vast coastline where you can catch a wave and also learn to surf. The only issue is that the waves are not consistent and also the surf will flop sometimes. You just need to be at the correct place at the correct time!
